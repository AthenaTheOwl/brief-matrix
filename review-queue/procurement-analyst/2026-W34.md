## intro

Two tariff dockets opened and one West Coast port slipped on dwell time.
The week reads as a continuation of the spring tariff posture rather
than a new direction; the supplier-side news is the actionable thread.

## items

1. USTR opened a Section 301 exclusion request window with a 30-day
   comment period for select Chinese imports
   ([USTR notice](https://example.invalid/ustr/section-301-2026-exclusion-notice)).
2. The Generalized System of Preferences renewal docket opened with
   notable carve-out language for critical minerals
   ([GSP renewal docket](https://example.invalid/ustr/gsp-renewal-docket-2026)).
3. Long Beach average container dwell time rose to 4.1 days from 3.6
   the prior week; PortWatch attributes the lift to a ground-handler
   staffing gap
   ([PortWatch bulletin](https://example.invalid/portwatch/long-beach-week-25)).
4. A working paper finds firms with single-source supplier
   arrangements absorb 2.3x the cost shock of dual-sourced peers
   when tariff regimes shift mid-contract
   ([POM early-access paper](https://example.invalid/pom/single-sourcing-tariffs-2026)).

## what-this-means

If your category sits inside the Section 301 list, the exclusion
window is the cheapest path to a 12-month duty break; the comment
period closes mid-July. The Long Beach dwell-time print does not
yet rise to a routing change, but it is the second weekly print in
a row above 4 days, and the academic finding above gives a frame
for why your single-sourced lines are the ones to inventory first.
